Voice Similarity Assessment

Dear participant,
In collaboration with Oxford Wave Research and the University of Cambridge, my MA project seeks to better understand the relation of voice similarity ratings by humans and by an automatic approach. In this experiment you will be presented with pairs of voice recordings and asked to judge the similarity of each pair. The recordings may be of the same speaker or of different speakers. Please provide some personal details before proceeding to the experiment.
Thank you!
Linda Gerlach, Philipps-Universität Marburg

User Information


Training Phase

The first three comparisons are training examples to familiarise yourself with the experiment. Each contains two short voice recordings. Rate the similarity of the voices on a scale from 1—9, where 1 indicates that they are very different, and 9 that they are very similar. Thus, a HIGHER number indicates a MORE SIMILAR voice. When assessing similarity, do not focus on the accents of the speakers, any non-speech noises, or any of the spoken content. Instead, aim to give your overall impression of how similar the voices sound.
